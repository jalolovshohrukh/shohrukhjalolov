A good week. Busy in the way that compounds rather than drains.

## CifarX

Most of my week went to **business development at CifarX** — partnership conversations that have been simmering for a month finally moved. The lesson I keep relearning: deals don't close in the pitch, they close in the patient follow-up nobody sees. Show up, be useful, repeat.

## Founders

Two long calls with founders I advise. One at **SoftClub**, one earlier-stage. Different problems, same root: distribution beats product more often than founders want to believe. A great product that no one can find loses to a good product everyone trips over.

> Build something people want, then do the unglamorous work of making sure they can find it.

## Refresh

Quiet progress on **Refresh**. Nothing flashy to report — and that's usually a good sign. The boring weeks are when the foundation gets poured.

## One thing I'm sitting with

Proximity to the market is underrated. Being *here* — in Dushanbe, close to the customers and the teams — is not a constraint to overcome. It's an edge. More on that another week.
